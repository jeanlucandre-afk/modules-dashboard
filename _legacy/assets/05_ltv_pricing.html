Pricing decisions — the reasoning

From the start, the plan for Still was to run it as a subscription. The reasoning was straightforward: the agency model resets revenue every month and every new client requires selling from scratch. A subscription means predictable income and a customer that keeps paying without needing to be re-converted. Looking at the numbers, a client on the €249/month Pro plan staying for 20 months generates €4,980 in lifetime value. That is more than 16 times what a one-off €299 agency project brings in. Once we saw that clearly, the subscription model was the only thing that made sense for Still.

For the pricing strategy we went value-based. Agencies doing similar work charge between €2,500 and €15,000 a month. AdCreative.ai, the closest SaaS comparison, charges €189/month but has no human creative layer and no Meta management built in. We are delivering more than both and charging less. The price is set by that gap, not by what things cost us to produce.

The attraction offer took a bit more thinking to land on. Our first instinct was a free trial, which felt like the obvious move. After going through it in class, we realised that free samples tend to pull in people who want something for nothing rather than people with a real problem and a real budget. We switched to the decoy offer: a €49 trial pack of three creatives. Low enough that someone interested will not hesitate, but with just enough commitment that it filters out the wrong people. The €49 also gets credited toward the first month, so the step from trial to subscriber feels like continuing rather than starting over.

Looking at all the data together, the case for this pricing structure is solid. At 5% monthly churn the average customer stays 20 months. Blended across monthly and Pro users, LTV sits at €2,600. Gross profit per customer after a 75% margin is €1,950. The LTV to CAC ratio against the €49 trial is 53 to 1, well above the 3 to 1 minimum that Disciplined Entrepreneurship Step 17 uses as a benchmark. The numbers confirm that the real growth lever for Still is not acquiring more customers at the entry price. It is moving customers from the €79/month plan to the €249/month Pro plan, where the lifetime value more than triples.
