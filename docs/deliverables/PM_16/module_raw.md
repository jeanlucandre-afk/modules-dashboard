## Module Description

Students who study this module will cover the topics of founding a legal entity, the associated tax requirements and strategies and options for external financing, and ultimately assessing which course of action is most appropriate for any given set of circumstances. Being a founder and entrepreneur is often seen as a paradise that is easily achieved. This module will debunk this myth and help students understand the real work, in clear steps, that get them through assessing the correct direction to take, and follow proven steps that have enabled CODE students to develop successful companies.

**General Guidelines**

In any assessment, three elements have to be demonstrated together:
- knowledge,
- application of knowledge,
- reflection on the application of knowledge.

### Knowledge Areas

Students who successfully pass this module have acquired the following knowledge and are able to use it in practice:
- Know standard approaches to developing a startup: Lean Startup, Disciplined Entrepreneurship, and Y-Combinator.
- Know the options for legal structures and taxation impact
- Know popular options for capital and fundraising options, where necessary.

## Standard Assessment Instructions

Students will apply topics covered in this module in their CODE projects and demonstrate the resulting learning progress during the semester. This demonstration of learning progress is structured in the following points, all of which are mandatory:

- Participation in workshops and learning units or a check-in with the module coordinator or another assessor.
- Completion of the guided reflection essay linked from the digital classroom in the teaching space on Notion.
- There is an optional oral exam of 15 minutes. If the assessor deems an oral exam is necessary, you will receive a message with a link to book an appointment for the oral exam. In the oral exam, you are expected to answer questions based on your hand-in.
