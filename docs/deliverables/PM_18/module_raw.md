Module Description
Is generative AI the next revolution for the product world? What happens next? Will we see different ways to find and deliver value to our users and their communities, and will we do so in fundamentally different product organizations? Regardless of how these questions will be answered and how AI evolves in the future, generative AI is here and profound impacts on product management and adjacent disciplines can already be seen. While generative AI gets most of the attention, we should not overlook the essential role product and data culture play in making it useful for product development.

Standard Assessment Instructions
This is a
portfolio assessment with optional oral exam
. You are expected to hand in concrete examples demonstrating your applied (not theoretical) responses to all of the questions mentioned in the knowledge areas. This must include at least one detailed, product- or operations-related prompt for an LLM of your choice, which must be accessible to the assessor (the assessor must be able to use the prompt). Your process of working with generative AI must become clear from this.
For each response, include a reflection on the potential for value creation and any risks you might see. The total length of the reflection part of the portfolio must not exceed 2,000 words. The descriptions of the responses, prompts, etc. do not count against this limit. All reflection must be individual work by you.
The hand-in must contain a statement on how generative AI was used in its creation.
Compile your portfolio into a single Pdf and hand it in
using this Google Form
by the announced deadline.
There is an optional oral exam of 15 minutes. If the assessor deems an oral exam is necessary, you will receive a message with a link to book an appointment for the oral exam. In the oral exam, you are expected to answer questions based on your hand-in.
