## Module Description

IMPORTANT: To pass this module successfully, attend the LU series and use the learning resources in the Google Classroom for additional self-directed learning to get prepared for the assessment.

Overall, this module helps students to develop an in-depth understanding of the product discovery process and equips them with practical skills to navigate through the early stages of product development. It provides an overview of the product discovery process based on Generative AI and HI (Human Intelligence) tools to define a problem worth solving, identify user needs, understand the market, generate ideas, and validate prototypes to develop a Minimum Viable Product (MVP). Starting with a clearly defined problem or opportunity, students learn how to design a product with a measurable impact based on hands-on group work activities. As part of the discovery process, students learn how to utilize methods for Customer Discovery, Market Research, Ideation, Prototyping, and the evaluation of product ideas.

### Qualification Objectives

Students who successfully pass this module are able to:
- Analyze different external factors to understand the market that influences a business
- Apply user research methods to identify user needs and generate user insights
- Apply creativity techniques to generate a wide range of ideas to solve a problem
- Create prototypes and user tests to validate concepts based on a hypothesis
- Evaluate solutions based on criteria such as desirability, feasibility, viability, and sustainability

### Knowledge Areas

- Problem/Opportunity identification and definition
- Customer Discovery & Market Research (Market including market size and market growth, external factors, target audience, and competitors)
- Solution ideation process and creativity techniques
- Collaborative product design and prototyping
- Solution/Product validation (desirability, usability, feasibility, viability, sustainability)
- Minimum Viable Product (MVP) definition & Build-Measure-Learn feedback cycle

## Standard Assessment Instructions

Disclaimer: In addition to participating during the LUs, students are expected to self-assess their knowledge and skills and augment their project experience with self-directed learning, e.g. utilizing the learning resources.

Students will apply topics covered in this module to their CODE projects/work projects and demonstrate the resulting learning progress during the semester. This demonstration of learning progress is structured in the following points, all of which are mandatory:

1. One presentation in the "Better Products" format. Alternatively, if the module coordinator is present when students are applying methods related to this module at other events, students must announce to the module coordinator that they want this to be part of the assessment at least one week before the event.

2. Oral exam (duration 25 minutes) with a presentation (duration: 10 minutes) including six examples of how each of the six listed knowledge areas was applied to their CODE project/work project, and a reflection of the effects on the product discovery process during the semester.

   To achieve a Level 1 the presentation must at least contain the application of the following Product Discovery tools in the CODE project/work project:
   - Problem statement
   - Summary of results of primary research (transcripts of at least 5 user interviews or survey results must be added in the appendix)
   - Summary of results of secondary research about users, market and competitors
   - Definition and quantification of the user group/beneficiary group
   - 1-3 detailed Personas
   - User journey map
   - Value Proposition Canvas
   - Competitor analysis with a competition matrix
   - Analysis of external factors with a PESTLE
   - Bottom-up and top-down TAM/SAM/SOM calculation
   - Prognosis of future market development
   - Definition of the beachhead market
   - Description of the solution derived from the application of creativity techniques
   - Value proposition statement
   - Solution evaluation based on desirability, usability, feasibility, viability, sustainability and a SWOT-analysis
   - Description/visualisation of the prototype
   - At least 3 hypothesis cards for the prototype test
   - Experiment board
   - Methods and results of the prototype test
   - Description/visualisation of the MVP including a user story map
   - Description of the individual Build-Measure-Learn Feedback Cycle

   A wider selection of suitable Product Discovery tools should be applied to achieve a Level 2, for example, a Lean Canvas, trend-scenario analysis, or a Solution-Impact Board.

3. Q&A (duration: 15 minutes) with knowledge & reflection questions to synthesize findings from the Product Discovery process and draw conclusions based on the results for current and future project work.

4. Hand-in of presentation slides with the content mentioned above, including a statement on how generative AI was used in the process. The presentation slides must be handed in as a single PDF document by the announced hand-in deadline via Google Form.

In addition to the above, students who want to achieve a Level 3 must write a reflection essay or choose one of these options:
- Write an article with the CODE/external community as intended audience.
- A workshop offered to the CODE/external community (can be a recorded video). Details to be discussed with the module coordinator.
