Module Description
STS is a general education built on the liberal arts, focussing on topics in philosophy, psychology, sociology, history, politics, law, economics, literature, and the arts. The Modules STS_02 (Academic Reading), STS_03 (Research) and STS_04 (Presentation) describe three different forms of assessment of such topics. The central focus in each of these modules is understanding and reflecting that topic well and with academic depth. In STS_04 this is achieved through tasks centering on presentation. For details about presentation please refer to the section on presentation in our
knowledgebase
.
Level Expectations
For a level 3 you should have read and watched all materials thoroughly and remember major and minor points from them. You should know and have thought through all the themes discussed in class, and come up with your own observations, criticisms, summaries, and ideas. If applicable, you should write a very good essay and be able to express yourself sensibly.
Gaps and flaws in each of these dimensions reduce the grade accordingly. If you do not have not at least a basic sense of the main points of the main themes of the class, and if you do not show at least a shred of independent thinking and good judgment, it's probably gonna be a fail.
No, there is no clearer way of saying this. Just be there, work through the mateirals, and think with us, then you gonna be fine.
Standard Assessment Instructions
For standard assessment you join a regular STS LU from the offers of the current semester, and follow all the instructions and requirements provided in the course description. Most LUs in STS can be used to satisfy STS_02, STS_03, or STS_04, but you need to check whether the respective module is possible for the LU you choose. Note that you can use each STS LU for only a single module assessment.
The module is typically assessed with a presentation or video essay. All specifics of assessment are determined by the lecturer of the chosen LU. They will provide you with the requirements for each possible module assessment in their LU at the beginning of term.
Note on AI
STS trains the mind, not your ability to use generative artificial intelligence. Therefore all assessments and much of the teaching are designed or required to be engaged in without the help of any generative AI tools.
Alternative Assessment Instructions
For Alternative Assessment, you can do your own \"Reading Project\" or \"Reflection Project\" (the former being about a text, the latter about a question) to be done mostly in self-study. For that you need agree with an STS lecturer on a topic within the range of STS, do at least two 15 minute check-ins with them, and create a deliverable. The grade is determined by both your performance in the check-ins and the deliverable. For details on how to register a Reading Project or Reflection Project, please refer to this
this page
. Note that you must apply for such projects with a specific STS lecturer at the beginning of term before the deadline.
All further specifics of assessment are determined by the lecturer supervising your Reading- or Reflection Project.
Early Assessment Instructions
Early Assessments are only possible with Alternative Assessments - you can simply do all the necessary steps and arrangements more quickly, if the lecturer agrees.
