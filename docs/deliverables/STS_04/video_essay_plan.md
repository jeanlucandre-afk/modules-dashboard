# STS_04 — Video Essay Plan

**Format:** 10-minute video essay, submitted as an MP4 link.
**Deadline:** 2026-05-04
**LU:** *The Critique of Technology*
**Anchor text:** Evgeny Morozov, *To Save Everything, Click Here: The Folly of Technological Solutionism* (2013).
**Allowed shortlist (lecturer-set, the only options):** Morozov · Andreessen, *Techno-Optimist Manifesto* · Zuboff, *Surveillance Capitalism* · Barbrook & Cameron, *The Californian Ideology*.

## What the lecturer is grading

Three things, equally weighted in the structure:
1. **Understanding of the book** — major and minor points, accurately represented, with quotes.
2. **Critique of the book** — where it's strong, where it overreaches, where it has aged well or badly.
3. **Your own opinion**, connected to STS-class themes (philosophy of technology, political economy, ethics, sociology of knowledge). Not a product pitch — a position.

The arc below maps to those three explicitly. No AI-use statement is required for this LU.

## Thesis (one sentence)

Morozov's solutionism critique lands hard on the surface of an AI ad-creative tool, but his blanket suspicion of "improvement" misses the harder question: *which* problems are real, and which ones did we invent so we could sell the cure.

That second clause is the personal twist. It comes from building Mango Lab.

## Talking-point bullets per beat

(*These are notes for you to write from, not lines to read.*)

### Beat 2 — Morozov in his own words
- Find and quote: his definition of solutionism (Introduction). Page-cite on screen.
- His move: solutionism doesn't just give bad answers, it picks the wrong questions.
- Pair it with his concept of *internet-centrism* — the belief that "the internet" is a coherent thing whose logic should restructure other domains.
- Pull one short quote (≤ 15 words). Quote cards on screen with page numbers — examiners notice.

### Beat 3 — proving it's not a dead critique
- Three real product taglines from current AI tools (yours optional). Each one demonstrates: a complex thing reframed as a tractable problem.
- Don't dunk. Just show the pattern.

### Beat 4 — applying it to AI ad creative
- The category's pitch: "Make ads in seconds." Morozov's question: was the bottleneck really seconds?
- What's actually being optimised: variant volume, brand-safe averages, CTR. None of those are "creative."
- One thing the tool genuinely makes possible (lowering the floor for a small business that could not afford an agency).
- One thing the category is solving cosmetically (the "I don't know what to say" problem is not a generation problem; it's a strategy problem).
- This beat is the heart of the essay. Spend the time.

### Beat 5 — the honest pushback
- Morozov's blanket suspicion makes the word *solutionism* do too much work. A coffee app and a predictive policing system are not the same thing.
- He underweights the agency of builders who *know* the critique and design against it.
- Calibrated solutionism: name the problem precisely, name what stays unsolved, ship the narrow thing.

### Beat 6 — the working principle
- "Whose problem was this?" as a default check.
- One specific design change. (e.g. removing a feature that optimised for a metric the user didn't actually care about; or refusing to add a "tone of voice" generator because the strategy work it papers over is the actual value.) Pick one real one. Concrete beats abstract every time.

## Sources to cite (use all four texts from the shortlist)

The lecturer gave a four-text shortlist. Using only Morozov leaves three unread on the table — that's fine for the *anchor*, but referencing the others lightly shows you engaged with the LU's whole reading.

1. **Morozov (2013)** — primary anchor. Cite at least 2 page-specific quotes (beat 2). One more in beat 5 where you push back against him.
2. **Andreessen (2023), *Techno-Optimist Manifesto*** — the steelman counter. Quote the strongest line in beat 5 — the one that, on its own, sounds reasonable. Then show why Morozov's critique still bites it.
3. **Barbrook & Cameron (1995), *The Californian Ideology*** — use in beat 5 as the deeper genealogy: solutionism didn't fall from the sky, it's the operating system of the worldview Barbrook & Cameron named 18 years before Morozov. One quote, on screen.
4. **Zuboff (2019), *Surveillance Capitalism*** — use lightly in beat 4 or 6 as the "what happened next" frame: the move from solutionism (problem-framing) to behavioural surplus (problem-harvesting). One sentence is enough; you're not arguing Zuboff, you're situating Morozov.

Plus optionally: Kranzberg's First Law as a closing epigraph card — "Technology is neither good nor bad; nor is it neutral." Useful as the final still frame.

## Watch-outs

- **Don't turn it into a Mango Lab ad.** The product is a case study, not the subject. If a viewer remembers the company more than the argument, you've failed.
- **Don't strawman Morozov.** Quote him directly. Beat 5's pushback only works if Beat 2 was generous.
- **No em-dashes** anywhere in the script (humanise rules). Full stops, commas, colons.
- **Read the book's intro and one chapter end-to-end** before scripting. Not skim. Quoting from a skim shows on camera.
- **AI-use statement.** Short, honest: "Used Claude to choose the anchor text from a four-text shortlist and to structure the argument. All reading, writing, recording, and editing done by me." Submit alongside.

## Production sequence to hit 2026-05-04

1. **Read Morozov.** Intro + Ch. 1 (or whichever chapter has the cleanest solutionism examples). 90 min.
2. **Skim the other three.** Andreessen full (it's short), Barbrook & Cameron's central thesis, Zuboff's intro. Mark the strongest quotable line in each. 60 min.
3. **Pull quotes.** Morozov ≥3 (beats 2 and 5), Andreessen ≥1, Barbrook & Cameron 1, Zuboff 1. 30 min.
4. **Write the script** to the beat sheet, in your voice. ~1,400–1,600 words for 10 min at conversational pace. **3 hrs.**
5. **Audit script** for em-dashes and prohibited words. 15 min.
6. **Record voiceover.** Allow retakes. 1 hr.
7. **Capture visuals:** product screen rec, quote cards, three taglines, the whiteboard sketch. 2 hrs.
8. **Edit.** 3–4 hrs.
9. **Export MP4 + upload, share link.** 20 min.

Total: ~12 hrs of work. Doable in a day if you start early.
