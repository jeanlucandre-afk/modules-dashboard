Who did you reach out to and what channel did you use? (e.g., "10 founders via LinkedIn," "10 students via email," "10 potential partners via a warm intro").

We did two things at the same time. The first was warm outreach, messages to friends asking if they knew anyone running paid ads for a business or an e-commerce brand. No pitch, just asking for a conversation. Something like "do you know anyone I could have a 15-minute chat with? I'm just trying to learn." The second was cold LinkedIn, split into two batches. Saturday we went after DTC founders, focused on creative fatigue. Sunday was e-commerce brand owners and freelance media buyers. Both batches used short connection requests under 300 characters, referenced something specific from their profile or a recent post, then followed up with one question once they accepted: "last time you needed new ad creatives for a campaign, how did you handle it?" No product mention until they described a real problem. We also ran LinkedIn Premium throughout, which made a real difference in who we could reach.


What was your response rate? (e.g., "I sent 10 messages and received 1 reply, a 10% response rate").

Roughly 25% across everything. Warm outreach came back fastest, which makes sense because there is a mutual contact behind the ask. Cold LinkedIn actually did better than I expected. The gap between a free account and Premium was real enough that I would not do another sprint without it. Three of the warm conversations turned into actual paying clients at the €40 trial tier. None of the cold outreach has closed yet but a few are still in conversation.


Reflect on the responses (or lack of responses). What did this experiment tell you about your message or your value proposition?

A few things stood out. Warm outreach worked almost immediately because a friend vouching for you is a completely different thing from a cold message. The three clients we got all came through that channel, which tells me the product is real. But it is also a ceiling, and we hit it fast.

On the cold side, Alberto's network did not respond to direct outreach but started coming back after we posted organic content on LinkedIn. They did not reply to the ask. They just appeared after seeing something. That tells me that audience wants to see the thing actually exist before they will talk.

The messages that got replies were the ones that led with something specific from their profile or a recent post. Generic openers got ignored. And the follow-up that worked best was the Mom Test question about what they had done before, not anything about what we are building. People will talk about what they have already done. They are not going to evaluate something for someone they just met.

25% on cold outreach is not a normal number. That told me the problem is real.


Based on this single experiment, what is the #1 thing you will change in your outreach message or strategy before you contact your next 10 customers?

The referral ask. At the end of every conversation, warm or cold, the next message should have been: "do you know anyone else who deals with slow creative turnaround or creative fatigue on Meta ads?" We did not do that consistently and we should have. Every person who described a real problem was basically a door to two or three more people with the same problem. We left that on the table. The next sprint is less about who else to message and more about treating the conversations we already have as a way into more.
