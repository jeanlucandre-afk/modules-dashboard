Who did you talk to and why did they fit your chosen beachhead market? (Be specific about their characteristics).

We talked to Fatema, the marketing coordinator at CODE University. Small team of five people doing everything, organic content, event coverage, website maintenance, merchandise ordering, and now with the one person who handled half of that leaving, she is taking all of it on herself. No dedicated designer. No proper equipment. They borrow a camera from someone else. She fits the beachhead market because she is exactly the kind of person we are building for: a small organisation trying to punch above their weight with almost no resources, where the marketer is doing five different jobs at once and none of them particularly well because there is just not enough time. The problem is not that she lacks skill. She knows what good looks like. She just cannot get there with what she has.


What was the most surprising or counter-intuitive thing you learned during your conversation? (Share a specific story or direct quote).

I expected her to tell me that creating content was the hard part. She does not even do ads. That whole side is two other people's job. Her biggest daily task is posting stories. That is it. The most basic, repetitive thing in the job is the one that drains her most because it never stops. She also told us she found out about a cultural evening the morning it was happening. That was the actual reason she described herself as overworked, not the volume of work but the total lack of planning. Things just appear. You show up and something new is on the list.

The quote that really hit me was almost at the end: "people use ChatGPT for marketing anyways, so why not have an app for that exact purpose?" She said it casually, like it was obvious. We were trying to sell her on the idea and she basically sold it back to us herself.


Did this conversation validate or invalidate your core assumption about the problem? How has your confidence in this problem area changed?

Validated in a general sense, but we were wrong about where the pain actually sits. Our assumption going in was that ad creation was the hard part, that people were spending hours making something that did not perform. For her, ads are someone else's problem entirely. Her pain is organic content, daily stories, event coverage alone with no equipment. There is a 40-second video she filmed last Monday that still is not posted because Marcel is the only one who edits and he is backed up.

So the core assumption held, content ops is genuinely painful and time-consuming, but the specific job we thought we were solving is not the one she needs solved. That shifted my confidence. I am more confident the problem is real. I am less confident we know exactly which version of the problem to go after first.


Based on this single conversation, what is the next question you need to answer? What is the next experiment or conversation you need to have to de-risk your idea further?

We need to talk to someone who is actually running the ads. In her team that is two other people we did not speak to. But more than that, we need to talk to an SMB owner who is doing all of this alone with no team. She has backup. Someone else handles the ads, someone else does the editing, there is a weekly meeting where tasks get divided. The person we actually need to reach is the one who is also the performance marketer, also the content creator, also the founder, all at once. What does that person's Monday morning look like when they have no Marcel to hand the video to? That is the next conversation.
