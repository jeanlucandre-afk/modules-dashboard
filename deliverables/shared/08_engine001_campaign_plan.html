SECTION_01 / OVERVIEW

Campaign Overview

Codename: ENGINE_001 — SaaS Acquisition Sprint.

The product: Mango Lab is a fully automated ads meter — a SaaS platform at mango-lab.de where marketers connect their ad accounts (Meta, Google, TikTok, LinkedIn) and the platform measures, monitors, and surfaces signal continuously. Free during beta. Replaces the weekly "open every dashboard, copy into a spreadsheet, write a Slack update" ritual.

Primary objective: Drive 30–60 sign-ups on mango-lab.de within 14 days, at a target cost-per-sign-up ≤ €15. Activation target: 50%+ of sign-ups connect at least one ad account within 24 hours.

Secondary objective: Build a re-targetable pixel audience of 5,000+ marketers and a CRM of every sign-up (with industry, company size, ad-spend tier captured at sign-up) for follow-up campaigns and product-led nurture.

Tertiary objective: Produce a publishable case study for the Marketing & Sales class — "How Mango Lab acquired N users in 14 days on €400, measured by the meter we were selling." The campaign IS the proof artifact.

Why 14 days, not 30 or 2. DACH B2B Meta CPMs run €10–18. At €28.50/day, Meta gets enough daily signal to push out of cold-start; at €13/day (a 30-day spread) the algorithm never escapes the learning phase; at €200/day for 2 days you waste 80% of spend on un-optimized delivery before the model converges. 14 days is the validation sweet spot AND the smallest credible window to call results.

SECTION_02 / POSITIONING

The Duality — How We Show Up

Mango Lab the product lives in the same tension as Mango Lab the brand: human in the framing, machine in the doing. Half the ads lead with the human element (the marketer's pain, the Friday-afternoon spreadsheet ritual). Half lead with the technical element (the meter, the data, the brutalist proof card). The contrast across the ad set is the brand.

VOICE_A

The Human Element

Warm. Direct. Spoken like a marketer who has built reports at 11pm. No SaaS-speak, no "synergize your ad spend," no enterprise gloss. Hooks land on the feeling of opening four dashboards and trusting none of them. Builds trust before it asks for an email.

VOICE_B

The Technical Element

Precise. Systematic. Speaks in numbers, latency, and dashboards. Mono type, all caps, data-card layouts. Treats the marketer as someone who already knows what CPL means. Closes on a metric, not a tagline.

Every ad in ENGINE_001 is scored against this axis before it ships. If a creative is "kind of both," it's neither — kill it.

Two-Phase Sprint

PhaseDaysAction
P1 — LEARN D1 → D5 Launch all 5 creatives at equal weighting. Hands off the ad set. Track CTR, CPS, and activation rate daily but make zero changes. Algorithm needs ~72h to find the responsive cluster.
P2 — CONCENTRATE D6 → D14 Pause any creative with CTR < 0.9% or zero sign-ups after 5K impressions. Duplicate the top performer with 2 variant headlines (same image). Keep ad set untouched — only swap creatives. Do NOT pause the ad set or the algorithm restarts learning.

Activation is the real conversion. A SignUp event without an OAuth connection is a bounce in disguise. Wire a second pixel event — AccountConnected — and treat it as the lower-funnel conversion. Meta will optimize delivery toward people who actually use the product, not just leave their email.

SECTION_11 / RISKS

Risks & Mitigations

RiskSeverityMitigation
Algorithm never escapes learning phase High Stick with one ad set, do not split. If SignUp volume is too low for algorithm signal, fall back to Landing Page View on day 1–3 then switch back to SignUp once volume builds.
Sign-ups happen but nobody connects an ad account High Activation is the real conversion. Wire the onboarding so the OAuth prompt is the first thing a new user sees post-sign-up. Send activation reminder email at +2h, +24h. Track AccountConnected as the secondary KPI.
Brutalist creative reads as "edgy" for cautious DACH marketers Medium Hedge: 3 of 5 creatives lead with the human / warm side. The brutalist proof cards run alongside, not alone. The ad set as a whole is balanced.
OAuth permission anxiety — "why does it need my Meta access?" Medium Read-only scopes only. Permission screen explains exactly which scopes (read ad accounts, read insights — never write). Show the SOC 2 / GDPR badges on the OAuth screen.
Spend exhausts in <14 days (Meta over-delivers) Low Lifetime budget caps total spend. If Meta wants to spend faster, it's a positive signal — let it run, document, plan ENGINE_002 with €1K.
Bad sign-up quality — students, freelancers exploring, not real prospects Medium Capture "monthly ad spend tier" as a sign-up field (€0–1K / €1–5K / €5–25K / €25K+). Filter the report by "qualified" tier (€1K+) when calculating CPS.
