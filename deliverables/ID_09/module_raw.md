Module Description
N.B. This module will be offered as 'Assessment Only' in Fall / Winter semesters.
This module explores the new and emerging role for design in industry and its contribution to product innovation and value proposition. It provides students with an overview of how to determine what to make and do, why to do it, and how to innovate contextually, both immediately and over the long-term. This further enables students to create meaningful products—services with character—by considering relevant touch-points. Thereby students learn techniques like market and brand analyses, future scenarios, technology forecasting and roadmapping as drivers for strategic design.
General Guideline
In any assessment, three elements have to be demonstrated together:
Knowledge
Application of knowledge
Reflection on the application of knowledge.
Qualification Objectives:
The individual student work is assessed holistically and is required to evidence the following learning outcomes/qualification objectives through the ability to:
Identify and utilize a range of methods to assist in the strategic development of your project (e.g. future scenarios, technology forecasting, storyboarding, user stories etc.)
Develop clear avenues for ‘creative outcome’ development (e.g. design product, service, experience, analog object) that are contextually appropriate
Create project development milestones and/or time plans that are contextually appropriate and acknowledge local/global developments (e.g. technological, political, social)
Present work in a creative, coherent and concise manner (which includes adherence to the word count, proper referencing and citation and the use of contextually located images).

Standard Assessment Instructions
The basis for the standard assessment is:
1. Work portfolio as a poster; 2. Poster Presentation; 3. A reflective self-assessment piece (approx. 500 words)
. What each element constitutes:
Work portfolio as a poster:
Your poster should represent your design process and reflections, while focusing on the learning objectives. The poster should guide the assessors through your assessment evidence in a structured and clear matter (a submitted collection of folders will not be assessed). A guideline for the poster format will be presented in the LU/via the LU Slack channel. The poster (in PDF format) should be uploaded to Fuxam prior to the Demo event: the date (and location on campus) will be shared in due course.
Please note: the below is meant as a guide to developing your posters, you have creative freedom to adapt as you see fit (ensure content of your poster work is aligned to the qual objectives of the module).
1st Poster:
An introduction/framing to the area/topic/project to be developed through Design Strategy and its contextual relevance (which includes using secondary sources and proper citation)
Your understanding of the new and emergent role of Design in Industry and its implications for your area/topic/project and the broader global context
Your investigation into and understanding of different design strategy tools/methods inc. their advantages, disadvantages, and implications of use
Your utilisation of a range of design strategy tools/methods in the development of your area/topic/project.
2nd Poster:
Your developed avenues for ‘creative outcome’ development (e.g. design product, service, experience, analog object) that are contextually appropriate
The next steps in development for the area/topic/project which includes project milestones and/or tome plans (this should be framed over the immediate, mid-term and long-term and reflect your understanding of local/global developments)
The next steps in the development of the project in relation to design outcomes and touchpoints that are contextually appropriate.
Poster presentation:
Your poster will be presented to an open audience/other students at a Demo event - therefore practice how you will be able to talk through your project process outcomes and process within a brief amount of time (c. 5-7. mins).
Please note: Failure to attend the session without extenuating circumstances will constitute a non-submission for this module.
A reflective self-assessment piece (approx. 500 words)
, containing:
Your self-assessed level and your reasoning behind it.
If the work submitted is made in a team, specify what you were responsible for.
A personal reflection, such as how happy you are with the outcome, your inspirations, main lessons learned (failings and challenges), or other factors that influenced your course of actions.
Reflections on your experience, key learnings, obstacles, etc. throughout the LU utilising reflective cycles such as Gibbs (reflective cycles/models utilised should be outlined and cited).
N.B. any imagery included should be properly cited (if secondary sourced), captioned (if authors own), and referred to within the document. All secondary sources (refs to papers/articles etc.) must also be properly referenced/cited within all documents in APA 7th. Parts 1) Work portfolio as a poster and 3) A reflective self-assessment piece (approx. 500 words) must be uploaded to Fuxam prior to the Demo event as per the hand-ins deadline for the semester on the Academic Calendar in Notion.
Alternative Assessment Instructions
Alternative Assessment:
You can submit a proposal for an alternative assessment via Fuxam, however it is your responsibility to get in contact with the module coordinator to discuss your proposal well before the alternative proposal deadline.
N.B. This is to ensure there is enough time for you to register for standard assessment if your alternative assessment proposal is rejected or if it actually falls within the standard assessment guidelines.
In which cases should you consider an alternative assessment?
There can be multiple scenarios that may require an alternative assessment, for example: Perhaps you would feel more comfortable showcasing your project work in a different format than that specified in the standard assessment instructions. Perhaps the project you have been working on throughout the semester would benefit from you showcasing your work in a different format (e.g., developing a short video to show your project work).
What could an alternative assessment look like?
You could propose being assessed via a 15 minute oral assessment. You could split the 15 minutes into 10 minutes to showcase your work in a structured manner, followed by 5 minutes of questions by the assessor/module coordinator. You could propose to submit a work portfolio of 2500 words (inc. visuals) that take the reader through your project process, outcomes and reflections.
