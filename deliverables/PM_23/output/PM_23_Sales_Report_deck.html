User persona: the marketing operator

Who they are

Lara, 32. Marketing lead at a 22-person B2B SaaS in Berlin. Reports to a founder who is not technical and does not want to be. Owns paid, content, and outreach. Has a €4k–€8k monthly Meta budget she defends every quarter.

A typical day
  • 09:00 — opens Meta Ads Manager, hunts for the worst-performing ad set
  • 11:00 — reviews booked-call calendar with the AE
  • 14:00 — debates a Notion doc on next month's content cadence
  • 18:00 — closes laptop without a clear answer to "is the budget paying off"

Daily pain

Five tabs open, three tools, no single read on whether the budget is working.

Weekly pain

The founder asks "what's the ROI" on Monday. She has no defensible answer until Thursday.

Quarterly pain

Budget review. She has to justify spend that has no clean attribution to revenue.

Buyer persona: the founder who signs

Who they are

Tomas, 41. Solo founder or founder-CEO of a 10–40 person SMB (B2B SaaS or marketing agency). Burned €2,000+ on an agency that delivered "a PDF and two Instagram ads". Now runs marketing himself or through one hire. Buying authority: full.

What he reads before he buys
  • Founder-led short-form video (this is where Still found him)
  • Two or three competitor reviews on G2 / Capterra
  • One LinkedIn post from someone he trusts
  • The Still landing page and its three social-proof logos
"I don't need another dashboard. I need to know if the €4,000 I just spent did anything."

Decision criteria (in order)

1. Is there a real human I can talk to in 20 minutes?
2. Can I see a current customer's setup?
3. Is the contract month-to-month?

Pains and jobs to be done

Pain 1 — Attribution gap

56% of SMB owners cannot reliably attribute revenue to marketing spend (Salesforce, 2024).

Pain 2 — Tool sprawl

Average SMB marketing team uses 5+ tools to run a single campaign. Context switches kill speed.

Pain 3 — No marketing hire

Founder is the marketing team. Tooling has to assume the operator is busy and not specialised.

Jobs to be done

  • Functional: "When I check on Monday morning, give me one number that tells me if last week's spend worked."
  • Emotional: "Make me look like I know what I'm doing in front of the founder."
  • Social: "Help me defend the budget at the next review without a slide deck."

Value-based proposition

Core promise

Still gives an SMB founder one weekly read on whether marketing spend is working, without hiring a marketing team.

Outcomes (not features)
  • Defendable budget. A founder can answer "is it working" in one number, on demand.
  • 20 hours of operator time back per month. The reporting work that filled Friday afternoons happens automatically.
  • Faster kill decisions. Bad campaigns get cut on day 3 instead of day 14, which compounds across the year.
  • One source of truth for the team. No more "which spreadsheet is current".

Qualification criteria · BANT

B — Budget

≥ €2,000/month already being spent on marketing today (paid, agency, or hire). If they're not spending, they're not buying.

A — Authority

Founder, CMO, or marketing lead with sign-off. We disqualify if a procurement gate is mentioned in the first call.

N — Need

They can describe the attribution gap in their own words within 30 seconds. If they can't, the pain isn't acute enough yet.

T — Timeline

They want a setup within 60 days. We park anything > 90 days into the nurture sequence.

Disqualification is a feature. A 4-pass BANT call should disqualify roughly 40% of booked calls in the first 5 minutes. That keeps the closer's calendar honest and the pipeline forecast trustworthy.
