PM_22 Slide Deck — Shotgun v2 — Editorial print register

Three takes. None of them feel AI.

Same brief, fresh wardrobe. Each direction draws from independent print magazine taste (Gentlewoman, Apartamento, MacGuffin/Offscreen DNA) and uses experimental or premium-paid typography that actually has personality. No orange. No cream-and-black-and-Mango-by-default. Pick one and we build the deck.

E

The Gentlewoman

Bodoni Moda Italic · Manrope · Oxblood + bone

Severe tabloid editorial. Big italic display serif at 100px+ headlines. Bone background, deep ink, oxblood as the single accent. Layout reads like a quality print quarterly: rules separating sections, pull quotes set in italic, three-column grids for argument-density. Mango orange is gone. Production version would use PP Migra Italic or Author Italic. Most "I take this seriously" of the three.

F

Apartamento

Caprasimo · Atkinson Hyperlegible · Terracotta + cobalt

Warm hand-feel editorial. Caprasimo as a fat distinctive serif (genuinely uncommon, not Fraunces). Terracotta as primary, cobalt as deep accent, warm bone background. Hand-drawn margin annotations. Loose grids that read like a curated independent magazine, not a startup deck. Production could swap Caprasimo for PP Editorial New or Migra Roman. Most personality of the three.

A pattern, surfaced six times.

In six interviews with SMB owners and marketers, one pattern came up unprompted in five of them.

An agency or freelancer is paid between €600 and €4,000. A nicely-designed PDF lands. A handful of static creatives are produced. Performance is opaque. The campaign ends and nobody can explain what worked.

Money exchanged. Clarity not.

From the field "I still have this PDF I never use." — Lisa, after €600 to a freelancer who delivered three statics + a brand guide. ROAS 1.2.

Chapter 04 / 06 p. 28
Knowledge area four

Collaborative
design and
prototyping.

A wizard-of-Oz first MVP. A €400 Meta sprint as the proof. Three clients. One pattern. Eight tests. Four killed. Two retired without conclusion. The remaining two are the wedge.

FONTS · DEMO uses Caprasimo + Atkinson Hyperlegible (Google Fonts free). PRODUCTION should swap Caprasimo for PP Editorial New (Pangram Pangram) or keep Caprasimo if you want fully free. Atkinson Hyperlegible is genuinely good and stays.

Wins on

  • Most personality of the three
  • Terracotta + cobalt feels curated, not corporate
  • Hand-drawn marginalia softens academic register
  • Caprasimo is genuinely uncommon — not a reflex pick

Gives up

  • Warmth could read as "informal" if grading rewards severity
  • Two-accent palette (terracotta + cobalt) is more to manage
  • Caprasimo is heavy — needs careful sizing
G

Velvetyne Maximum

Director · Compagnon · Moss + sulphur

Maximum distinction. Director (Velvetyne, free, fully variable display) does the heavy lifting. Compagnon (Velvetyne, free) for body. Deep moss green background, sulphur yellow as the brave accent, warm bone for cards. Reads like the work of an independent design studio that has its own taste. Highest risk + highest reward of the three. If Velvetyne CDN fails, falls back to Spectral and Tenor Sans. Production can stay on Velvetyne — they are fully open-source.

06.

Build.
Measure.
Learn.

ENGINE_001. €400 META sprint. The chapter where every theoretical claim from chapters 03–05 ran into reality. Some claims survived. Some did not. The ones that survived are the wedge.

FONTS · Director and Compagnon both Velvetyne (fully free, open-source). Loaded from velvetyne.fr CDN — if blocked, falls back to Spectral + Tenor Sans visually but loses some character. PRODUCTION can stay 100% Velvetyne.

Wins on

  • Most distinctive deck in the room. By a lot.
  • Director typeface alone makes this unforgettable
  • Moss + sulphur is a palette you do not see in AI defaults
  • 100% open-source font stack (no licensing risk)

Gives up

  • Highest risk of "trying too hard" if execution slips
  • Velvetyne CDN reliability is not as strong as Google Fonts
  • Sulphur yellow is divisive — readers either love it or do not
  • Most demanding direction to keep coherent across 50 slides
