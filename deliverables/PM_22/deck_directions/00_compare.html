PM_22 Slide Deck — Visual direction shotgun

Pick a direction.

Four distinct visual systems for the Product Discovery hand-in deck. Each shows three sample slides at full 16:9 fidelity (cover, content, section divider). All four respect the constraint set: no Fraunces / no Inter / OKLCH-friendly palettes / double-bezel where used / macro-whitespace / no side-stripe borders / no gradient text. They differ deliberately on vibe, typography, and layout discipline. Pick one and we build the full 50-slide deck against it.

A

Lab Notebook

Reforma 1969 · Hanken Grotesk · Warm paper

Editorial restraint with marginalia. Reads like a quality independent journal or a serious lab notebook. Off-white paper background, italic display serif for emotive moments, generous footnotes, hand-feel sticky notes used sparingly as marginalia. Mango orange shows up only on accents and the dark section dividers. This is the most "academic" direction without being boring. Most likely to make Swantje go "this person actually cared about the craft."

KA1 · Problem statement

The agency PDF problem we kept hearing.

Across six interviews with SMB owners and marketers in DACH, one pattern came up unprompted in five of them.

An agency or freelancer is paid €600 to €4,000. A polished PDF of brand guidelines is delivered. A handful of static creatives are produced. The campaign runs. Performance is opaque. Nobody can explain what worked.

The money is exchanged. The clarity is not.

Black Press Editorial

Editorial brutalism. Almost zero decoration. Black ink on warm white, hairline rules, serious italic display serif, three-column layouts that respect text density. KA1 · Problem statement Page 04

Five of six interviews surfaced the same structural failure mode unprompted.

The pattern

Money exchanged, clarity not. An SMB pays an agency €600–€4,000 for a "campaign." A polished PDF lands. A handful of static creatives ship. The dashboard is opaque. Nobody knows what worked.

Where it appeared

Lisa Bauer, solo DTC matcha founder, paid €600 for three statics + a brand guide. ROAS 1.2. Fatema's bakery friend, €2,000 for a brand discovery + two ads. Outcome unknown.

What it implies

The agency model fails because the deliverable is opacity dressed as polish. The wedge for Still is not faster creative. It is visible iteration. The SMB will pay for what the agency hid.

D

Field Manual

Spectral · Hanken Grotesk · JetBrains Mono · Reference book

Swiss/Bauhaus reference book aesthetic. Cool graphite paper, hairline rules, numbered chapters, mono labels, three-column technical layouts. Spectral serif for headings, monospace for citations and disclosures. Mango orange shows up only as section markers and one statistic per slide. KAs are numbered like manual chapters (01–06). The deck reads like a 1970s engineering reference document that earned its precision. Best fit for "I want this to look like a serious technical document, not a pitch deck."
