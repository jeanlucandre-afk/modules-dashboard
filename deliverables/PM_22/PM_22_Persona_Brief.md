## 3. Persona profiles (synthetic 3–6)

### Persona 3 — Marc Hoffmann
**Business:** Voltura — early-stage B2B SaaS, time-tracking + invoicing for German freelancers and small consultancies. Munich.
**Age:** 34
**Role:** Co-founder, CEO. Doing growth himself; technical co-founder is heads-down on the product.
**Stage:** ~50 paying customers, €8k MRR, 18 months in. Bootstrapped, no VC. *Sized against SaaSRanger 2024 (R9): median bootstrapped SaaS hits $1k MRR at 12–18 months, $5k by year 2–4. Marc at €8k MRR sits above the median but credibly so.*
**Team:** Marc + 1 technical co-founder + 1 part-time customer support. No marketer.
**Spend:** Tried Google Ads (€500/month for 3 months, ROAS unclear), is now experimenting with LinkedIn Ads (€800/month, 6 weeks in). At LinkedIn Lead Gen Form CPL of $60–$120 for SaaS mid-market (R7), €800 buys him roughly 7–13 leads a month. Considering Meta Ads but worried about audience match for B2B.
**Trial conversion:** Voltura runs a 14-day opt-in trial. Currently converts at 14% trial-to-paid. *Below OpenView 2022 opt-in median of 25% (R4) — a number Marc knows and is anxious about.*
**Channels:** SEO (slow but working), LinkedIn organic posts (his own profile, ~6k followers), some podcast sponsorships.
**AI usage:** Uses ChatGPT and Claude daily for product copy, blog posts, customer-support drafts. Comfortable with prompts. Has tried 3 different "AI ad creative" tools and bounced off all of them, too generic, did not fit Voltura's voice.
**Time on marketing:** 10–15 hours per week, fragmented around product calls and customer support.
**Budget for tools:** Will pay €50–€150/month for a tool that demonstrably saves 4+ hours per week. Has a pattern of subscribing, using for 2 months, cancelling if no clear ROI.
**Frustration:** Says LinkedIn Ads is "the only B2B channel that actually performs" but the creative iteration loop kills him. He writes copy, builds an asset in Figma, runs it, gets data after a week, iterates. Two-week cycle per learning. *Aligned with Meta's own 2023 finding (R11) that creative fatigue compounds quickly and refresh cadence of 2–4 weeks is the industry rule.*
**Decision authority:** Full. Spends from a single bank account.

**Pain hypothesis to test:** Marc is the cleanest fit for Still's ICP. We want to test whether the iteration-cycle pain (2-week loops on LinkedIn ads) is felt strongly enough that he would pay €100/month to compress it.

### Persona 4 — Sven Krüger
**Business:** Krüger Performance — small Hamburg-based performance marketing agency. 8 active retainer clients + 2–3 project clients per quarter, all SMBs in DACH. Specialties: Meta Ads + Google Ads.
**Age:** 41
**Role:** Owner. Started as a freelancer 6 years ago, built up to a team of 4 (Sven + 2 ad managers + 1 designer who works remote from Lisbon).
**Revenue:** ~€340k/year. Average retainer: €2,800–€4,500/month per client. *Sized against Foxwell Digital 2023 (R6): US boutique agencies $1,500–$3,000/month, mid-market $3,000–$8,000, EU pricing 15–20% below. DACH boutique tier maps to €1,500–€4,500/month.*
**Pain points:**
- Designer is the bottleneck. Every creative request goes through him; backlog of 10+ creatives at any time.
- Client churn at the 12–18 month mark, not the 6-month mark people assume. *Focus Digital 2024 (R10): small agencies (1–10 staff) average 32% annual churn, equivalent to ~3-year client tenure on average. Sven's lived experience matches that, "they stay about a year and a half then quietly drift."*
- Onboarding a new client takes 3 weeks before any ad is live: brand discovery, asset gathering, creative production.
- Constantly fighting fee compression. Clients say "we found someone cheaper."
**AI usage:** Personally uses ChatGPT for client emails. The team has no AI workflow. Sven is curious but suspicious. He tried one AI creative tool, the output was "obviously AI-generated" and he would not show it to clients.
**Buying behaviour:** Will pay for tools that increase team output. Already pays for Meta Business Suite Pro features, a creative asset library, and a reporting tool. Total agency software spend: ~€800/month.
**Decision authority:** Full.

**Pain hypothesis to test:** Sven is the agency-channel persona. We want to test whether he would use Still as an in-house creative tool (replacing some of the designer's work) OR whether agencies would resist a tool that "could replace them," competitive versus co-opted.

### Persona 5 — Anja Becker

**Age:** 29
**Role:** Marketing manager. Reports to the COO. Has a budget but every spend over €500 needs approval.
**Team:** Anja + 1 content marketer + a freelance designer on retainer. 3 people total for marketing.

**LinkedIn Ads economics:** At LinkedIn's $60–$120 SaaS Lead Gen CPL (R7), Anja's €6k/month buys roughly 50–100 leads. Trial conversion sits near 12%, below the OpenView opt-in median of 25% (R4), which is why she is reading every article she can on landing-page optimisation.
**Frustration:** The freelance designer is a bottleneck (sound familiar?). Anja has ideas weekly that die because she cannot get assets made. She has tried creating creatives herself in Canva but they "look like Anja made them," slightly off-brand.
**AI usage:** Uses Notion AI heavily, ChatGPT for research, has not tried AI creative tools yet but is open. Read about Midjourney but the COO is sceptical of AI imagery for "trust-sensitive" B2B.
**Reporting pressure:** Has to present monthly to the leadership team. Needs to defend every channel's CAC. CAC payback target of 18 months sits inside the OpenView median band (R5: 16–24 months for $1–5M ARR cohort).

**Pain hypothesis to test:** Anja is the constrained-decision-maker persona. We want to test whether Still's price point (~€100–€200/month) lands inside her unilateral authority OR whether sales motion needs to target the COO.

### Persona 6 — Tobias Ahrens
**Business:** Tobias Ahrens Consulting — solo B2B sales-training consultant. Sells a 6-week online program (€2,400) and 1:1 coaching (€350/hr). Cologne.
**Age:** 38
**Role:** Sole proprietor. Everything is him.

**Marketing:** LinkedIn organic content is the engine. Posts 3x/week on LinkedIn, has 12k followers. Runs LinkedIn Ads to a lead magnet (a free 12-page PDF on enterprise sales), then nurtures via a 7-email sequence.
**Spend:** €600/month on LinkedIn Ads. At LinkedIn Lead Gen CPL of $60–$120 for B2B (R7), €600 buys him ~5–10 leads a month, which feeds his sales pipeline at roughly 1–2 program conversions a month at €2,400 each. Considered Meta but believes "my audience is on LinkedIn." Pays €99/month for an email tool, €30/month for a landing page builder.

## 4. Interview structure (used by Phase B for all 5 synthetic interviews)

Each interview follows a Mom Test format — no pitching, behaviour-focused, past-tense questions where possible. ~8–12 question/answer exchanges per transcript.

**Standard question arc (interviewer's playbook):**

1. Walk-me-through-your-week (current marketing reality)
2. What does making a piece of creative actually look like for you (deep on workflow)
3. Tell me about the last time you tried [paid ads / agency / tool] (past-tense behavioural)
4. What did you do after that? (consequence question)
5. Where does most of your marketing spend go — time and money? (resource allocation)
6. What would have to be true for you to try [the thing they avoided] again? (threshold question)
7. Is there anything you have tried that actually worked, even a little? (positive deviance)
8. (Optional follow-ups based on persona) — agency angle for Sven, leadership-approval angle for Anja, lead-magnet angle for Tobias, etc.

**Closing:** A short reflection section on the interviewer's side noting: what was confirmed, what was contradicted, what new question this raises.

## 5. What we expect to learn (testable hypotheses going into Phase B)

| # | Hypothesis | If TRUE we expect | If FALSE we expect |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | The "PDF deliverable" agency failure mode is a generalised pattern | At least 3 of 5 synthetic personas describe a similar agency-disappointment story unprompted | Only Lisa repeats the pattern; others have different agency stories |
| H2 | The blocker is visibility/control, not budget | Personas with budget say they'd spend more if they could see what's working | Personas say they're at their budget ceiling regardless |
| H3 | The 2-week creative iteration loop is a strong pain | Marc, Anja, Tobias each describe a multi-day delay between creative idea and deployed test | They describe creative as an afterthought, not a bottleneck |
| H4 | Agencies (Sven) would use Still as a tool, not resist it | Sven describes designer-bottleneck pain that aligns with Still's value | Sven views Still as competitive — would not use it |
| H5 | Mid-market marketing managers (Anja) need executive buy-in for tool purchases | Anja's tool-purchase decisions go to the COO above €500/month | Anja has full unilateral authority — pricing strategy unaffected |
| H6 | Service-business funnels (Tobias) are within Still's scope | Tobias's pain pattern matches the SaaS personas | Tobias's needs diverge meaningfully — service-business is out-of-scope for first ICP |

These hypotheses become **hypothesis cards** in KA5 of the slide deck.

## 8. AI-use disclosure (will appear in the deck and on each synthetic transcript)

> Five of the six interviews in this appendix are synthetic. They were generated using Claude (Anthropic Sonnet 4.6 / Opus 4.7) by running a dual-agent workflow: one agent acts as the interviewer asking Mom-Test-format questions, a second agent acts as the persona answering in character. Personas were grounded in (a) the real Fatema interview, (b) cited research benchmarks listed in §2 of this brief, and (c) the existing Mango Lab customer profile work in the validation plan v3 and traction test cards. The synthetic interviews were used to pressure-test patterns from the real interview and to surface variation across the beachhead market. They are clearly labeled and not presented as real customer testimony.
