## PESTLE — sourced data points (use these on the slide)

### Political / Regulatory

**P1 — EU AI Act, Article 50 (transparency obligations on AI-generated content)**
- Source: EU AI Act Article 50 ([artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/)), in force from 1 Aug 2024, transparency obligations apply from 2 August 2026
- Quoted: *"Providers of AI systems… shall ensure that the outputs of the AI system are marked in a machine-readable format and detectable as artificially generated or manipulated."*
- Why it matters for Mango Lab: AI ad creatives must be labelled. Watermarking and metadata become mandatory. Penalty exposure up to €15M or 3% of global turnover (Bird & Bird, 2026: [twobirds.com](https://www.twobirds.com/en/insights/2026/taking-the-eu-ai-act-to-practice-understanding-the-draft-transparency-code-of-practice))

**P2 — EU Code of Practice on AI-Generated Content (1st draft, Dec 2025; final June 2026)**
- Source: European Commission, Digital Strategy ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/.../code-practice-ai-generated-content](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/code-practice-ai-generated-content))
- Quoted: *"a multilayered approach combining visible disclosures with invisible or machine-readable techniques (such as metadata or watermarking)"*

**P3 — Digital Services Act (DSA) advertising transparency**
- Source: European Commission ([digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/.../dsa-brings-transparency](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/dsa-brings-transparency))
- Quoted: *"clearly indicate to users in real time that the information they see is an advertisement,"* disclose *"on whose behalf the advertisement is presented"* and *"the main parameters used to determine the recipient."*
- SMB scope: Smaller online platforms still face *"notice-and-action obligations and ad transparency duties"* (Oxford IJLIT 2025: [academic.oup.com/ijlit/article/...](https://academic.oup.com/ijlit/article/doi/10.1093/ijlit/eaaf004/8133991))

**P4 — TTDSG / TDDDG (Germany cookie consent law)**
- Source: Securiti.ai legal guide ([securiti.ai/blog/german-ttdsg-guide](https://securiti.ai/blog/german-ttdsg-guide/))
- Quoted: *"Violating cookie consent requirements under Section 25 of the TTDSG will attract a maximum fine of EUR 300,000"*
- New layer (April 2025): German Consent Management Ordinance ([usercentrics.com](https://usercentrics.com/knowledge-hub/cookie-flood-control-consent-management-ordinance-tdddg/))

## Honest limits (must caveat in deck)

1. **DACH SMB-specific ad spend doesn't have a public segmented number.** Statista and Grand View give Germany totals; the SMB share is unpublished. Use Germany totals as TAM proxy with caveat.
2. **Marketing-budget % of revenue (4.1%)** is from Bitkom across all German firms, not SMB-only. Directionally useful, not perfect.
3. **In-housing trend** is US-sourced (ANA / eMarketer), no equivalent DACH benchmark. Flag clearly when referencing.
4. **McKinsey State of AI** is global, not DACH. Use as direction-of-travel, not a regional figure.
5. **AI ad-creative market sizing** — no analyst publishes a standalone "AI ad creative" segment. Use the broader AI-in-marketing figure (Grand View) plus the AdCreative.ai acquisition as a directional signal.
6. **AI Act SMB penalty exposure**: headline cap (€15M / 3% turnover) per Bird & Bird; SMEs benefit from Art. 99(6) proportionality, but no DACH-specific implementing regulation published yet.
7. **Paywalls:** eMarketer SMB Infopack 2024 and full WARC in-housing report are paywalled. Only summaries quoted.
